About Gwynedd Maldwyn

Gwynedd Maldwyn is a large, predominantly rural constituency stretching from the Cambrian Mountains to the edge of Snowdonia, covering towns such as Machynlleth, Bala, Dolgellau, and Newtown. It brings together the historic counties of Montgomeryshire and Meirionnydd. The area is known for its natural beauty, sparse population, and strong Welsh linguistic and cultural identity.

Key Stats

  • Gwynedd Maldwyn is formed by merging Dwyfor Meirionnydd with Montgomeryshire and Glyndŵr.
